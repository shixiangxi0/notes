# About Dual World Theory

This site documents **Dual World Theory**: an evolving attempt to understand how complex systems persist while continuously absorbing change.

---

## Two Basic Terms

In this theory, a **system** is any bounded entity that must persist through time while exchanging change with its surroundings. A living organism, an organization, a game server, a database, even a civilization can all be understood as systems in this sense.

A **world** is the total field of change that a system has to face.

Without a system, there is only undifferentiated change, with no inside or outside. Without a world, a system has no pressure, no input, and nothing to maintain itself against.

> **A system is the interpreter of the world; the world is what it must interpret.**

| Perspective | What the system sees |
|---|---|
| Looking outward | External World: noise, entropy, open-ended possibility |
| Looking inward | Internal World: facts, causality, history, order |

---

## Two Worlds

Any system that persists through time effectively faces two worlds at once.

### External World

> The external world is governed by the second law of thermodynamics: **entropy increases and disorder spreads.**

To the system, the external world is not directly intelligible. It does not arrive with built-in facts or obvious causality. It appears instead as overlapping change, correlation, contingency, and noise.

The external world does not explain itself. It does not tell the system which changes matter, which are invalid, or which happened first.

And yet it is also the source of everything. Energy, input, opportunity, and threat all come from there. **Infinite possibility lies hidden within chaos.**

### Internal World

**The internal world is the order a system extracts from external noise.**

Through interpretation, adjudication, and propagation, the system turns disordered change into a traceable history of facts. Only in that way can it:

- Form a continuous identity
- Share a stable reality with other systems
- Temporarily preserve structure inside an entropic universe

**Dual World Theory explores how these two worlds divide labor and cooperate to keep a system coherent over time.**
